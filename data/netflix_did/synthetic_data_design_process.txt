The team discussion around the synthetic recommendation dataset follows a clear causal design
process rather than random feature generation. The process starts by listing every variable
that should appear in the data, then drawing a DAG that describes how those variables affect
each other, then writing the corresponding factorization of the joint distribution, and finally
choosing a sampling distribution for each conditional term. This is important because it means
the synthetic data is not only convenient test data. It is intended to reflect an explicit
causal model. The discussion connects variables such as minutes viewed, age, recommendation
system assignment, device type, genre preference, subscription type, major releases, and
baseline engagement. That makes the synthetic dataset a teaching tool for both simulation and
causal reasoning.
