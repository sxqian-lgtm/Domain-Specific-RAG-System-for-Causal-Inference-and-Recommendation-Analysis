Using the provided synthetic data, the average viewing pattern suggests a negative DID
estimate for the recommendation system. In the treated group, average minutes viewed move
from about 45.401 before treatment to 44.361 after treatment, a before after change of
about minus 1.040. In the control group, average minutes viewed move from about 44.487 to
44.687, a change of about plus 0.199. Subtracting the control trend from the treated trend
gives a DID estimate of about minus 1.240 minutes. This result means the treated group's
viewing time fell relative to the control group's trend over the same period. The estimate
should still be interpreted through the lens of the DID assumptions, especially parallel
trends and the absence of group specific shocks. But as a project note, it provides a
clear baseline finding for the RAG system to retrieve.
