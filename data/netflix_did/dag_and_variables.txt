The Netflix presentation defines a simple causal structure for the recommendation study.
Covariates include age, genre preference, and major release context. Treatment is a binary
indicator for whether the user was exposed to the recommendation system. Outcomes are
minutes viewed before and after treatment, represented in the project as baseline viewing
and post treatment viewing measures. The presentation explicitly notes that treatment
assignment is non random, which means raw differences between treated and untreated users
cannot be interpreted causally without adjustment. This variable design is a good teaching
example because it mirrors a common product analytics setting: user features affect both
who gets a feature and how much content they consume. A domain specific RAG assistant can
use this structure to explain why covariates matter and why a causal design is needed.
