One useful project idea from the team notes is that each DAG corresponds to a factorization of
the joint distribution of the variables. For a simple example with age, recommendation-system
assignment, and minutes viewed, the graph implies that the joint distribution can be written as
the outcome conditional on age and recommendation system, multiplied by the marginal
distribution of age and the marginal distribution of treatment assignment. This matters because
it forces the team to think concretely about how the synthetic data is generated. Instead of
treating the CSV as a black box, the project uses the DAG to determine what variables depend on
what other variables, and then chooses distributions accordingly. This strengthens the causal
story behind the data and makes the project more defensible than a purely ad hoc simulation.
