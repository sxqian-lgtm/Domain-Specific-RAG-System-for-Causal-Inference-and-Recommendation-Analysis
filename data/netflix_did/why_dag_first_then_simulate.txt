The project notes highlight an important modeling principle: first define the causal graph,
then simulate the data. This reverses the usual estimation workflow. In ordinary statistical
analysis, the analyst starts with observed data and estimates parameters. In synthetic data
construction, the team instead postulates conditional distributions, chooses parameter values,
and then forward-simulates observations from those distributions. This process is valuable for
learning because it forces the team to articulate the causal assumptions behind each variable.
It also creates a cleaner bridge between causal diagrams, probability factorization, and the
final dataset used for DID analysis. In short, the synthetic data is useful not only as test
data but as a structured way to understand the causal system itself.
