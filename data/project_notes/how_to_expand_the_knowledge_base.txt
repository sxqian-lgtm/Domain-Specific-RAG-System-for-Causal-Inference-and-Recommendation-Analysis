The best way to expand this project's knowledge base is not to dump more raw files directly
into retrieval. The better workflow is to keep source materials in raw_private, read them for
new concepts, then convert the useful parts into short topic-specific text chunks stored under
causal_methods, netflix_did, or project_notes. A good new chunk should answer one question
clearly, explain why the concept matters, and connect back to the current project when
possible. This workflow improves retrieval quality because the index stores focused concepts
instead of noisy raw pages. It also keeps the public project repository cleaner and easier to
explain. In practice, this means new PDFs, slides, or team notes should be treated as source
material for chunk creation, not as the final retrieval layer themselves.
