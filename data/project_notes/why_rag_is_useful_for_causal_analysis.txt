A domain specific RAG system is useful for causal analysis because the questions analysts
ask are repetitive, assumption heavy, and easy to answer inconsistently from memory. A
single workflow may need definitions of DID and IPW, reminders about ATT versus ATE,
project specific variable descriptions, and interpretation of synthetic or experimental
results. By retrieving curated project notes and method summaries before generation, the
system can produce answers that are more grounded and more consistent with the team's
actual analysis design. This is especially valuable in recommendation projects, where the
same business question can mix product context with methodological concerns. RAG does not
replace causal design, but it can improve the quality and speed of explanation, review, and
communication.
