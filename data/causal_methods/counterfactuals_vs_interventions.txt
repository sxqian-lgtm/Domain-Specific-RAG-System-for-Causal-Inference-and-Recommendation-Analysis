Interventional and counterfactual questions are related but not identical. An interventional
question asks about the outcome distribution if a treatment were set to a value across a
population or policy scenario. A counterfactual question asks what the outcome would have been
for the same unit, given what is already known about that unit, under a different action.
This means counterfactuals condition on factual evidence in a way that standard intervention
queries do not. In applied work, the difference matters when analysts move from policy
evaluation to case specific explanation. A DID estimate may answer the average policy style
question, while a counterfactual framework is better suited to questions about why a specific
outcome happened or how an individual case might have changed under a different decision.
