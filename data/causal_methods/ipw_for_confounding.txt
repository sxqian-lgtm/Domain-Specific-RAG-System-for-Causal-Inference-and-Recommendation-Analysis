Inverse probability weighting, IPW, is a causal adjustment method that reweights
observations so the treated and untreated groups look more comparable on observed
confounders. The basic idea is to estimate each unit's probability of receiving treatment
given covariates, often called the propensity score, and then assign larger weights to
units that were unlikely to receive the treatment they actually got. This creates a
weighted pseudo population in which treatment is less entangled with observed covariates.
IPW is useful when treatment assignment is non random but the analyst believes the key
confounders have been measured. It does not solve bias from unobserved confounding, and it
can become unstable when propensity scores are very close to zero or one. In practice, IPW
is often paired with overlap checks, weight trimming, and balance diagnostics.
