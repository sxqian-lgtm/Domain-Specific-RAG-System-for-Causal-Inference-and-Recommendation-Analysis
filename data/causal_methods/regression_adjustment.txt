Regression adjustment estimates treatment effects by controlling for observed covariates in
a predictive model for the outcome. In a causal workflow, the point is not only to predict
minutes viewed or conversion, but to block backdoor paths from confounders into treatment
and outcome. For recommendation analysis, a regression might include user characteristics,
baseline engagement, subscription status, and contextual variables such as major releases.
This can reduce bias when treatment assignment depends on those observed factors. However,
regression adjustment still relies on correct covariate selection and adequate functional
form. If important confounders are omitted or nonlinearities are ignored, the estimated
treatment effect can remain biased. Regression adjustment is often easier to explain to
stakeholders than more specialized methods, but it should still be justified with causal
reasoning rather than treated as a purely statistical fix.
