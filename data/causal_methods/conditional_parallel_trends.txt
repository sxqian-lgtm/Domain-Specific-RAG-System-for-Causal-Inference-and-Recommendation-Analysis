Conditional parallel trends is a refinement of the standard DID assumption. Instead of assuming
that treated and control groups would follow the same untreated trend on average, it assumes
that they would do so after conditioning on observed covariates. This is often more plausible
because units with the same covariates may be better comparisons than the raw groups overall.
For example, in a recommendation project, similarity may depend on prior engagement,
subscription type, age, or content preference. Under conditional parallel trends, the DID logic
still works, but the comparison is made within comparable covariate profiles. This idea also
opens the door to regression-based and weighting-based DID estimators that explicitly use
covariates to improve identification.
