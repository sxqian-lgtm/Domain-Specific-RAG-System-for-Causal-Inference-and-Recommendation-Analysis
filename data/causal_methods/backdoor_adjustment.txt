The backdoor criterion is a graphical rule for selecting adjustment variables when estimating
the causal effect of a treatment on an outcome. A valid adjustment set blocks every path from
treatment to outcome that starts with an arrow into the treatment, while avoiding adjustment
for descendants of the treatment. Intuitively, the goal is to remove confounding without
contaminating the estimate with post treatment information. Once a valid set is found, the
analyst can use the adjustment formula to combine outcome predictions within covariate strata
into an estimate of the interventional quantity. This is one of the central ideas in Chapter
3 because it turns a graphical condition into a practical estimation strategy. In a
recommendation setting, covariates such as age, prior engagement, or subscription status may
be used for adjustment if they block confounding paths and are not downstream consequences of
the recommender itself.
