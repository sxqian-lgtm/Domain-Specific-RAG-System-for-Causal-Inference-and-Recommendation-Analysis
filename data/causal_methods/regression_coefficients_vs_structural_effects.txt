One subtle lesson from the primer is that regression coefficients are not automatically the
same as causal or structural effects. A regression slope measures association after whatever
conditioning is implicit in the model specification. A structural parameter, by contrast,
describes how the outcome would change under a causal intervention in the data generating
system. These quantities coincide only under specific assumptions. This distinction matters in
linear models because analysts often read a coefficient table as if every coefficient were a
causal effect. Chapter 3 pushes students to separate predictive fit from causal meaning. In a
recommendation project, a positive coefficient on the recommender variable does not by itself
prove that the recommender increased viewing time. The interpretation depends on whether the
chosen controls block confounding and avoid post treatment bias.
