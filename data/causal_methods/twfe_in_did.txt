The two-way fixed effects model, often abbreviated TWFE, is a common regression framework for
DID. It includes a group fixed effect, a time fixed effect, and an interaction term between
post-treatment timing and treatment-group membership. Under the basic two-period setup, the
coefficient on the interaction term recovers the DID effect. The appeal of TWFE is that it
translates the DID design into a simple regression specification that is easy to estimate.
However, the conceptual justification still comes from the DID assumptions, not from regression
machinery alone. In modern applied work, TWFE is also discussed critically because more complex
multi-period or staggered-treatment settings can produce misleading estimates if the treatment
effect is heterogeneous over time.
