The do operator represents an intervention that sets a treatment variable to a chosen value,
rather than merely observing that value in the data. This distinction is crucial in causal
inference. The quantity P of Y given do X asks what would happen to the outcome if the analyst
or decision maker actively imposed X. That differs from P of Y given X, which is only an
observational association and may still reflect confounding. Chapter 3 uses this idea to
show why causal effect estimation is fundamentally different from standard prediction. In
practice, when a team asks what would happen if a new recommender were deployed, they are
asking an interventional question, not an associational one. The do operator provides the
formal language for answering that kind of business question.
