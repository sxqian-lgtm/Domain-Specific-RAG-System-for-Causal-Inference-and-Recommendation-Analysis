A collider is a variable on a path where two arrows meet head to head. Without conditioning,
a collider blocks association along that path. But once the analyst conditions on the
collider, or on a descendant of the collider, the path can become active and induce a
spurious association. This is why conditioning is not automatically helpful in causal
analysis. Some variables should be adjusted for, but others should be left alone. Collider
bias is especially relevant in observational product analytics because analysts often
condition on variables that are consequences of multiple upstream factors, such as selection
into a study sample, engagement thresholds, or post treatment filters. A good causal answer
must distinguish confounders, which often should be blocked, from colliders, which often
should not be conditioned on.
