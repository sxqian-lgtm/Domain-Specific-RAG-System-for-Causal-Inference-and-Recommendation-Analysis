DID can be estimated not only through regression but also through weighting. In the
covariate-adjusted DID setting, weighting methods use the propensity score to reweight the
observed changes over time so that treated and control units become more comparable. This can be
preferable when the propensity model is simpler or more trustworthy than directly modeling the
outcome trend. The basic idea is similar to IPW in standard causal inference, but now the
weighted quantity is often the change from pre to post. This matters because it gives analysts
more than one estimation path under conditional parallel trends. In practice, weighting-based
DID is useful when the team wants a less restrictive alternative to a heavily parameterized
regression model.
